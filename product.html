netplex. is emulation, execution and automation in one event-driven platform. Here's the whole thing - the canvas, the consoles, the pipeline, the automation toolbox and the fabric.

The canvas is the product

A topology you'll actually
want to look at.

Premium, flat, dark-first. Drag nodes, draw links in four styles, annotate, and watch live packet flow stream across the fabric - all on a circuit-board canvas built for engineers reading dense screens at 2am.

  • Live link flow - see traffic move, not invented Gbps numbers
  • Export to SVG, PNG (2×) or JSON - Visio / draw.io friendly
  • Presentation mode - laser, pen, arrows, shapes & spotlight over your live topology · Architect+

The image pipeline

Drop an image. Walk away.

EVE-NG made you SSH in, juggle files and run fixpermissions. netplex. does the whole thing - convert, set permissions, organise, boot-test - and shows you every step. No CLI.

Auto-classify

Matches the upload against 140+ device kinds across 70+ vendors and gives it a human name - never vios-adventerprisek9-m.SPA…

Disk-guarded

Refuses an upload before it fills your disk: "Only 8.2 GB free - needs ≥10 GB." A precise, actionable message, never a stack trace.

Boot-tested

Every image boots headless before it's marked Ready. If it fails, you get what happened, why, and what to do - plus "email me the fix."

140+ device kinds · 70+ vendors

Bring your own licensed images.
We handle the rest.

From core routers to next-gen firewalls and white-box NOS. Alpine MicroVM ships free and built-in; vendor images are user-provided, and the pipeline classifies, converts and boot-tests them automatically.

All vendor names and marks belong to their respective owners and are shown to indicate supported device definitions only.

The stitch - distributed fabricRoadmap · beta

Two machines.
One topology.

The headline differentiator nobody else does. A single lab spans several cheap PCs and cloud VMs - netplex. stitches their virtual switches together with VXLAN tunnels, so a link between nodes on different machines just works.

Distributed multi-chassis VXLAN is on the roadmap and currently in beta. Single-host emulation, automation and the full Architect toolbox ship today.

  • Scheduler places nodes by free CPU / RAM across hosts
  • A host dropping degrades only its nodes - the lab keeps running
