FAQ

Getting started
What do I need to run netplex.?
A 64-bit Linux host with KVM available (/dev/kvm), 4 vCPU and 8 GB RAM to start, and 20 GB free disk plus room for your images. Running in a VM works if nested virtualisation is enabled. There's no client to install - everything is in the browser.
Is there really a free tier?
Yes - the Community tier is free forever: 20 nodes per lab, the React canvas, live terminal, packet capture and a read-only API. It's genuinely useful for CCNA and CCNP study.
Migrating from EVE-NG
Can I import my existing EVE-NG labs?
Yes. Import an EVE-NG .unl, a GNS3 .gns3project or a containerlab YAML in about thirty seconds. Nodes, links and interface assignments carry over.
Do I still need to run fixpermissions?
Never. The image pipeline converts, sets permissions, organises and boot-tests every upload automatically, and shows you each step. No SSH, no CLI.
Pricing & licensing
How much cheaper is annual billing?
Annual is roughly two months free: Plus is $99/yr (vs $119.88 monthly), Pro is $199/yr (vs $239.88), Classroom is $449/yr per instructor. Switch the toggle on the pricing page to compare.
Will a licence blip lock my running lab?
No. Enterprise uses MAC + TPM fingerprinting with a 30-day grace period on hardware migration, and netplex licence rekey opens a support ticket automatically. Reliability beats DRM zeal.
Are students free?
Yes - individual access is free with a verified school email. Classroom ($449/yr per instructor) covers an instructor-run, graded environment for up to 50 student pods.
Features
Is the multi-host VXLAN stitch available now?
The distributed multi-host VXLAN stitch is on the roadmap and currently in beta. Single-host emulation, the canvas, consoles and the full Pro automation toolbox ship today.
Which vendors are supported?
140+ device kinds across 70+ vendors - Cisco, Juniper, Arista, Fortinet, Palo Alto, Nokia, MikroTik and more. netplex. ships the device definitions; you supply your own licensed images and the pipeline does the rest.
